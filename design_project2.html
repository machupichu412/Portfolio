LEGO Star Wars App Design Project

2022

Problem Statement

LEGO Star Wars collectors want a way to track the market value of different LEGO Star Wars items so that they can make better financial decisions when purchasing LEGO.

Audience

LEGO Star Wars items are resold across many different platforms. It is difficult for LEGO collectors to track the value of different items, especially after they have retired and do not sell for retail value anymore. LEGO is an expensive product to collect, so finding good deals can be crucial to saving money while also enjoying the hobby. This product is designed for passionate collectors of LEGO Star Wars items. This product’s target market is those who exchange LEGO Star Wars items, whether that be buyers, sellers, or both. While niche, creating a product for this market is valuable as there recently has been an emergence of LEGO Star Wars “investors”, who will purchase sealed products and resell them after they retire from retail stores. There is a lot of value in the LEGO Star Wars resale market, with retired and in-demand sets possibly having resale values of thousands of dollars. It could be potentially viable to offer a subscription-based model to access more detailed market data, as serious collectors would find value in this information.

Design Process

Starting the design, I knew that I wanted the item listings to be the main feature of the product. These listings would display price information of LEGO Star Wars products. I started off by making a rough draft of the item listing pages in Figma. The listings include an image of the item, the current market price, and a graph of the item's resale history.

Prototyping the functionality of the product probably took the longest time to complete. I learned to use Figma components in order to streamline the design process and easily use the same element across different pages, such as the bottom navigation bar. This saved me a lot of time as I did not have to recreate basic functionality across each new page.

For the user interface design, I wanted to pay homage to the LEGO Star Wars video games, mainly LEGO Star Wars The Complete Saga and The Skywalker Saga, as they are very popular in the LEGO Star Wars community.

I think the design inspiration is very obvious in the prototype itself. The logo is composed of the LEGO Star Wars The Complete Saga logo and uses the same font. In addition, the login menu and many more pages use the menu font from TCS. The Skywalker Saga inspiration is visible in the "hologram" style that is prevalent throughout the app, which is reminiscent of the Skywalker Saga pause menu. Holograms are very essential to the Star Wars aesthetic, so I think it ties the overall visual theme of the app together nicely.

User Feedback

Feedback during the design process suggested that there was some confusion with the functionality of the app, especially when first signing up. To address this, I developed a short tutorial that guides new users through the app.

The tutorial covers most of the features of the app, which hopefully improves the onboarding experience. I would like to perform more user testing with this tutorial in order to gauge its efficacy.

Solution

My product answers the problem statement by providing a resource for LEGO Star Wars collectors to find market data in order to make more informed financial decisions when deciding what, when, and where to purchase LEGO Star Wars items. This product would theoretically have every LEGO Star Wars item ever released and their corresponding price information available to view. Each listing for these items shows its current value based on items currently for sale online. Current for sale listings from across the web for each item are aggregated and put in one place, so that users of the app can find good deals on an item they want. In addition, the historical price information would be available in the form of a line chart, allowing users to see trends in the price and potentially predict future trends.

The product also includes a "collection" feature that allows users to estimate the total value of their collection by adding items to a list. I felt this would be a useful feature because as a LEGO Star Wars collector myself, I know it is a tedious task to attempt to find the price of every single item in my collection.

Future

Going forward, I hope to develop this prototype into a functional mobile or web application. I would like to utilize the Bricklink (LEGO resale marketplace), eBay, and Mercari APIs in order to actually track current and previous market data for every LEGO Star Wars item.

Retrospective

This design project was a huge learning experience for me. I learned a lot about the design process and the importance of user feedback. My knowledge of Figma definitely increased from the project, and I am now more proficient in the use of components to create designs.
